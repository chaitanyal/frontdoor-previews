The Challenge

The website did not fully represent the physician behind the practice.

Nothing about the original website changed the quality of care. The issue was first impression. Prospective patients had to work harder than necessary to understand who Dr. Dronavalli is, what he treats, and how to request care.

03

Next Steps Needed Clarity

Patients needed clear appointment, portal, contact, office, and resource paths from any device.

The Solution

A medical practice website built around trust, comprehension, and action.

FrontDoor Health redesigned the experience around the questions patients ask before scheduling: Is this the right specialist? What does he treat? How do I prepare? How do I request an appointment?

Physician First

The new site centers Dr. Dronavalli's pulmonary and sleep medicine expertise instead of treating the physician profile as secondary content.

Clear Care Areas

Conditions and services are organized for fast scanning, including asthma, COPD, sleep apnea, and pulmonary testing.

Patient Preparation

Patient resources and questionnaires are surfaced in a compact section before office details and FAQs.

Mobile-First Flow

Appointment, portal, phone, location, and hours information are easier to find on small screens.

Why It Matters

Better patient experience starts before the appointment.

A high-quality practice website does more than look modern. It helps patients orient themselves, understand fit, prepare for care, and contact the office with less friction.

Preview Model

The practice could evaluate direction before committing.

The project began with a working preview, not a static proposal. That gave the practice a concrete way to judge the design, content structure, and patient flow before deciding on next steps.
